Historical Sites Around Coimbatore

 

Marudhamalai Hill Temple

Marudhamalai Hill Temple is among the most renowned attractions of Coimbatore. This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The 12th century temple is surrounded by numerous trees, plants and herbs with hills in the backdrop. Devotees throng to the shrine in large numbers on occasion of festivals like Thai Poosam and Tirukarthigai.Situated on Western Ghats, Marudhamalai Temple is situated at a distance of 14.1 km from the Coimbatore railway station.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM to 8:30 PM

It is famous for the largest mercury based lingam. .The shrine is linked with numerous sages and siddhas, who worshipped at the centre. Major highlights of the shrine are decorations on the natural granite. As per folklore, it took 2000 years to build this temple. The shrine was constructed with bricks and mud mortar along with herbal additives, sand and lime.Dhyanalinga Yogic Temple is situated on the foothills of Velliangiri ranges surrounded by dense green forests, which is accessible by roadways. It is 29.1 kilometers from coimbatore.

Eachanari Vinayagar Temple

The Eachanari Vinayagar Temple is located at a distance of 13 km from Coimbatore on the Pollachi road. Built in 1500 AD, Eachanari Vinayagar Temple is one of the oldest pilgrimage centers dedicated to Lord Vinayaga. The idol at the Eachanari Vinayagar Temple is one of the largest ones and measures up to 6 ft in height. Inscriptions at the temple confirm that the idol was brought in from Madurai and was originally meant for the Perur Patteshwarar temple. The whole family can take part in the special offerings on special days like Chathurthi, Amavasya, Poornima and Karthika. Though open on all days throughout the year, the best time to visit the Eachanari Vinayagar Temple is during the Hindu festivals like Vinayaka Chathurthi, Thaipoosam, Saraswathi Pooja, Deepavali, Tamil New Year Day and Karthika Deepam when offerings like Homam, Abhishekam, Alangaram, and Deepaaradhanai are done here.Eachanari Vinayagar Temple is also known for its special vahana pooja conducted on new vehicles. As Lord Vinayaga is considered as the obstacle remover, a lot of devotees bring their vehicles here. Mahalakshmi Temple can also be visited while at Eachanari. It is 13.7 kilometers from coimbatore.

Visiting Timings:-5 am to 10 pm

 

The ISKCON Temple

The ISKCON Temple in Coimbatore is a well known pilgrimage centre located in the heart of the city. The ISKCON temple aims at spreading the science of Krishna consciousness with the help of Bhagavad Gita. Spread across an area of 20,000 sq ft, the temple has an array of beautiful deities belonging to Sri Radha Krishna, Sri Jagannath, Sri Baladev, Subhadra Devi and Sri Prabhupada. Built in typical Tamil architecture, other than the separate sanctums present for the different deities, the temple complex has a gallery showcasing the different founders of ISKON movement. Gift shops in the temple complex are the right places to purchase spiritual souvenirs for your loved ones.Though open on all days of the year, the best time to visit the ISKCON temple is during the festivals of Gaura Purnima, Rama Navami, Narasimha Chaturdashi, Varaha Dvadashi, and Sri Krishna Janmashtami which are celebrated in a lively and colorful manner with a lot of music and dance.This tranquil temple is strategically placed close to the city, at a mere 7 km, and autos and buses are easily available. One can pay a visit to the Codissia Trade fair complex which is another attraction situated nearby. It is 10 kilometers from coimbatore.

Visiting Timings:-4 am to 8:30 pm

 

Perur Siva Temple

Perur Siva Temple is a landmark temple in Coimbatore. This Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Siva is located 7 kilometers to the west of Noyyal River. The deity of this temple is considered'Swayambu Lingam' that is, self emerged. However, the greatest attraction here is the 'Kanaka Sabha', built in Dravidian architecture and adorned by a gold plated statue of Nataraja. Families or a person who enjoys architecture and history will enjoy the visit to the Perur temple. However, the best time to visit the temple would be during the festival of 'Panguni Uthiram'. The festival is celebrated in its full spirit during the month of March. The temple is open to devotees for worship from 05.00 AM to 12.00 PM and 03.00 PM to 08.00 PM every day. As the temple is usually crowded on holidays, it is highly recommended that you do not visit it then.This temple is a perfect spot for a few minutes of emotional relaxation. Being mere 16 min from the city, taxis, buses and autos are easily even quite early in the morning. Sri Maasaniamman Temple, Perur Lake and Post Colony church are close by and can be visited before or after the visit here. It is 8.4 kilometers from coimbatore.

Visiting Timings:-5.00 AM to 12.00 PM and 03.00 PM to 08.00 PM

 

Infant Jesus Church

One of the most visited Churches in Coimbatore city is the Infant Jesus Church. It was set up in 1984 to cater to the growing demands of the Catholic families and has become a prominent church due to the belief in cures and miraculous healing. The Church is open on most days while Mass are held on Thursdays and Sundays. It is 11.6 kilometers from coimbatore.

Visiting Timings:-6:30 AM -6:30 PM

 

The St. Antony's Church

The St. Antony's Church is located at Puliakulam, just 4 kms from the Coimbatore city. It is a very famous church where a large number of devotees arrive from nearby places. Originally built as a small chapel in 1859 by Fr. De Gelis, the church is now famous and stands tall with many facilities like separate floor for presbytery, open air shelter, religious depot with many articles and backyard for pilgrims.The church is open to all irrespective of caste and religion. In fact, this Church attracts a lot of devotees belonging to various caste, religion and language especially on Tuesdays. On Tuesdays, four holy masses are held here, followed by St. Antony's novena. The feast of St. Antony is celebrated every year on 13th June.People can easily travel to the shrine, as there are frequent bus and other means of public transport to reach here from various parts of Coimbatore city. Marudamalai Temple, Dhyanalinga Temple, Eachanari Vinayagar Temple, Patteeswarar temple, Parambikkulam, Aazhiyaar Dam, Kovai Kuttralam and Black Thunder Theme Park are some other attractions situated in the vicinity. As the summers in Coimbatore are hot and humid, the best time to visit the place is from November to March. It is 6 kilometers from coimbatore.

Visiting Timings:-5.00 AM to 12.00 PM and 03.00 PM to 08.00 PM

 

Kottai Hidayathul Islam Safia Jamath Mosque

Kottai Hidayathul Islam Safia Jamath, is a renowned Muslim place of worship at Kottaimedu. It is located just a kilometre away from Coimbatore railway junction between the Town Hall and Ukkadam. This is the first and the biggest mosque to be built in Coimbatore during the reign of Tipu Sultan in the 17th century, who is also considered to have offered prayers at the Kottai mosque. Islamic pilgrims from the entire country visit this mosque.Apart from the intricate architecture and its obvious service as a payer hall, the mosque is famous for imparting education. It also works as an information centre and dispute settlement agency. An Arabic College is a part of this mosque and functions efficiently maintaining good standards.Though it is a religious place, it is open to everyone. Tourists and students can visit the place to learn more about religion as well as history. Kottai mosque can be easily accessed from any part of Coimbatore, as it is situated right in the middle of the city. Marudamalai Temple, Dhyanalinga Temple, Eachanari Vinayagar Temple, Patteeswarar temple, Parambikkulam, Aazhiyaar Dam, Kovai Kuttralam and Black Thunder Theme Park are some other attractions situated in the vicinity. It is 650 meters from airport.

Visiting Timings:- 6am - 7pm
