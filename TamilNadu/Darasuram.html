Historical Sites Around Darasuram

 

Pazhayarai

Pazhayarai is considered as an ancient capital of the mediaeval Chola Dynasty and is around 4.2 km from Darasuram. This town also has various villages like the Nandipura Vinnagaram, Patteeswaram, Harishchandrapuram and the Thirusatti Mutram. The place is believed to be the home to four ancient temples like Metrali, Vadathali, Kelthali and Thenthali.

Pazhayarai Someswarar Temple is the prime attraction of this town, which is around 3 km away from Darasuram. The place can be reached easily by availing regular buses and mini buses from Darasuram. It is 2.9 kilometers from Darasuram.

Visiting Timings:-7:30am-12pm and 4pm-7pm

 

Tirunallur

Tirunallur is a village is popular for the Kalyanasundaresar Temple. The village is located in the Orathanadu taluk of the Thanjavur District and is around 8 km from Darasuram.

The Kalyanasundaresar Temple is located in the village of Nallur and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The lingam placed in the temple is worshipped by the devotees. It is believed that the temple was constructed during the rule of the Medieval Cholas. It is 61 kilometers from Darasuram.

Visiting Timings:-6am-1pm and 4pm-8pm

 

Papanasanam

Papanasanam is a panchayat town, which is around 10.8 km from Darasuram in the Thanjavur District. This town is famous for the temple of Pallaivanatha Swamy, which is believed to have been constructed by a Chola King.

It also houses a granary, which is 86 ft wide and has a height of around 36 ft. Having a capacity of about 3,000 kalam, it was constructed with lime stone by the Nayaks between 1600 and 1634. Moreover, this granary has been declared as a monument by the State Archaeological Department.

Another temple which is famous for housing 108 Shiva Lingams can also be visited. It is 13.4 kilometers from Darasuram.

Visiting Timings:-6:30am-1pm and 6:30pm-8pm
