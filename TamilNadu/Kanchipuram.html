Historical Sites Around Kanchipuram

 

Kanchi Kamakshi Temple

Dedicated to goddess Parvati, this Temple is an excellent piece of art during the period of Pallava kings in seventh century. Later on this temple was rebuilt in 14th century by the Chola dynasty. Kamakshi Amman, the another name of maa Parvati, believed to be swaroop of shakti. This place is also famous as Shakti peeth. The famous saint Shankaracharya, Kamakoti teertham hails from here. This place is a very religious importance.It has four colorful gopuras and the main sanctum has a gold plated roof. This important Devi temple, is one among the many temples situated in one of seven sacred city, as popular Kanchipuram is known. It has a graceful attraction, for all the visitors. It is 0.8 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-6:30 AM -8:30 PM

 

Ekambareswar Temple

Sri Ekambareswar Temple is considered one of the most popular and largest temples in Kanchipuram. It is situated on a huge area and is dedicated to Lord Shiva, in the form of Prithvilingam.The temple comprises an intricately carved 1000-pillared hall, 5 spacious corridors, massive outer stonewall and a 57 metres high Gopuram. Some 1,008 Shivalingams adorn the inner walls of the temple. A 3,500 years old mango tree is located in the courtyard that yields four different kinds of mangoes. It is 1.6 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-6:30 AM -8:30 PM

 

Sri Kamakshi Amman Temple

Sri Kamakshi Amman Temple is dedicated to Kamakshi, an incarnation of Goddess Parvati. Within the ‘Gayatri Mandapam,’ the sanctum is situated below the gold-plated vimana and is supported by 24 pillars that coordinate with the 24 words of the ‘Gayatri Mantra’.The deity of the temple is postured to hold a staff, noose, sugarcane bow and flower arrows in her hands. A shrine of the temple is dedicated to Adi Sankaracharya, who placed a chakra before the deity.A mango tree is situated outside the main temple and this is considered to be the place where Kamakshi worshipped a Shivalingam that was made of sand and gained Lord Shiva’s hand in marriage.In the month of February or March, the famous annual Car Festival is held at this site. During this car festival, the Goddess is taken out on a procession throughout the town. It is 0.8 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM -8:30 PM

 

Sri Kailasanathar Temple

Sri Kailasanathar Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and the main shrine is surrounded by some 58 smaller shrines. It is considered one of the simplest temples of Kanchipuram as it lacks any such ornamentation.The temple is constructed in red sandstone and its walls are adorned with fresco-style paintings and sculptures of lions; Parvati, wife of Lord Shiva and Shiva in various postures and forms. It is 2 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM -7:00 PM

 

Sri Varadaraja Perumal Temple

Sri Varadaraja Perumal Temple is situated atop the Elephant or Hastigiri Hill and is dedicated to Varadaraja Perumal or Lord Vishnu. The deity is enshrined within the temple tank and is carved out of atti wood.Once in every 40 years, the deity is brought out before the public for some 48 days. During the months of May and June, the annual Garudothsavam Festival is celebrated at this site and is attended by several people.The temple comprises of numerous sculptures and carvings of lizards that are plated in gold and of riders on horsebacks. The most interesting feature of the temple is the twin lizards that are crafted in silver and gold and are known to annul the sins of those who touch them.It also includes a 16th century pillared pavilion, 100-pillared hall that is carved out of monolith and ornamental rings at the four corners that are carved out of a single stone. It is 4.5 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM -6:30 PM

 

Devarajaswami Temple

It is believed that the temple first built by King Nandivarman, though originally it was built and expanded by Cholas, and main gopuram Gateway Tower is 130 feet high and seven tiered, in 14th century, is now situated on Gandhi Road, is the sacred town's main Lord Vishnu Temple. The chief deity is a form of Lord Vishnu, known as Varadaraja, means King who bestows the benediction. It is believed that the temple stands on the site where Lord Brahma performed a yagna, sacrifice, to invoke Lord Vishnu's presence. Among the temple's jewels there is a Necklace presented by Robert Clive, which adorns the deity during the Garuda Festival. An ancient architect Vishwakarma Sthapathis skill architecture is significant, this temple is famous for many things especially a stone chain which was sculpted from a single stone. It is a great divine destination and a great attraction as thousands of devotees are coming to have divine insight. It is 20.2 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-6:30 AM -8:30 PM

 

Thennangur Panduranga Temple

This temple is situated in Thennangur, Tiruvannamalai, built in 15th century, and famous for its unique design too, just 6KM on Vandavasi highway, away from Kanchi and 110 KM from Chennai, this village also described as a birthplace of Goddess Mahalakshmi. The temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna, lovingly known as Lord Panduranga, whose magnificent idol of 12 feet high made of stone portrayed standing, a majestic appearance. In spite of main deity there are main God, Goddess are present in the temple. Its main gate is Pallava style and rest structure resembles the design of Puri and Jagannath, a unique architectural masterpiece. It has a 16 pillared Hall, one feels heavenly while entering, and Lord Garuda at the centre space, is also a major attraction. This temple is having attraction for orthodox and non orthodox Hindus, and its magnificent structure for all kind of tourists. It has a 120 feet high tower, with 9. 5feet Gold Kalash and on top a sudarshan chakra. The Saffron flag on the tower, which attracts the devotees. It is 35.7 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-9am to 5pm

 

Tirumukkudal

Tirumukkudal is a popular pilgrimage destination offered by Karur, which is situated about 4 km away from Walajabad. The name ‘Tirumukkudal’ stands for mukkudal or the union of three sacred rivers - Palar, Cheyyar and Vegavati.

Tirumukkudal is recognized as a heritage site by the Archaeological Survey of India. This is an ancient temple constructed during the reign of early rulers of the Thondaiman Dynasty. The temple is dedicated to Lord Agasthiswarar and his consort Anjanakshi.

Pazhayaseevaram is yet another famous temple situated in the vicinity of Tirumukkudal temple, which is dedicated to Lord Lakshmi Narasimha. It is 23 kilometers from Kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-8:30 AM to 11 AM and 5:30PM to 8 PM.

 

Jain Temples

Two 9th century Jain temples are located at Tirupparuthikkundram and were mainly built for the Jain Tirthankaras. While one shrine remains inaccessible, the other shrine comprises a huge image of Mahavira and a number of frescoes.These temples were built in 9th century, even this sacred city was considered sacred for Jain's scholar too. Lord Mahavir is the main deity in one temple, and other is dedicated to Tirthankara, even few Pallava Kings were attracted by Jainism. This place is situated about 76 KM away from Chennai and close to Kanchipuram. These temples and deities including all articles are well preserved. These ancient temples are of great interest for Jains and other tourists too find an attraction. These are famous for special art and architecture, mural paintings, fresco and lot. It is 55.4 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-9:00 AM -6:00 PM

 

Sri Vaikunda Perumal Temple

Sri Vaikunda Perumal Temple is a Vaishnava temple and is known for its inscriptions and sculptures. The inner walls of the temple are extensively decorated with lion-shaped pillars. The lion-shaped pillars are believed to be the forerunners of the 1000-pillared halls that are found in later temples. It is 28.9 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM -6:30 PM

 

Sri Subramaniya Swami Temple

Sri Subramaniya Swami Temple is situated within the city and is considered to be a very popular temple for Hindus. The temple is renowned for the ‘Soma Skandha’ posture in which Lord Shiva has Goddess Shakti seated to his left and their son, Lord Subramanya between them. It is 72 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM -6:30 PM
