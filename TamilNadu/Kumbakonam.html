Historical Sites Around Kumbakonam

 

Uppiliappan Temple

Great temple for worship. One can enjoy the Chola architectural beauty. Well maintained throughout the centuries. It is believed that praying here would help good understanding between husband and wife. Tolerance level would be improved.This temple is 5km away from Kumbakonam. This is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The food served to God is prepared without salt, because this god is Uppiliappan. This is one of the famous ancient historic temple of Kumbakonam. It is 7.4 kilometers from kumbakonam.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM - 1:00 PM and 4:00 PM - 9 PM

 

Allagammai Kashi Vishvanathar

Allagammai Kashi Vishvanathar is a holy place, believed to have been constructed by a Pandya king in Chautallam. The presiding deities of this religious site are Subramanya and Vishwanath. The major attractions of this temple are the musical pillars and a seven-tiered mahagopuram. It is 1 kilometers from kumbhakonam railway station.

Visiting Timings:-6am to 12pm and 4pm to 9pm

 

Airavata Shiva Shrine

Airavata Shiva Shrine displays the Dravidian style of architecture and is named after Airavata, a white elephant. It is often believed that he was an ardent devotee of Lord Shiva. The temple houses various sculptures and paintings and was built with the concept of the nitya-vinoda

The temple has an idol of Goddess Deva Nayaki along with various carvings of interwoven legs of females with a head at the centre. As per legends, Lord Airavateswara cured Yama, who had been cursed by a Rishi. He told Yama to take a bath in the sacred tank, Yamateertham, to get rid of the curse. This tank is considered as holy among devotees. It is 4.5 kilometers from Darasuram.

Visiting Timings:-7am-12pm

 

Darasuram Temple

This is a Shiva Temple some 4 km West of Kumbakonam in Darasuram. This is maintained by archaeological department of India. The temple is well-maintained and has even a park around it. The timings of this temple is from 6-12noon and in the evening 4-9. So the between hours can be spent in the park or with the unique beautiful historical sculptures inside the temple. This is one of the must see places of Kumbakonam. It is 4.5 kilometers from kumbakonam.

Visiting Timings:-7AM to 12PM

 

Adi Kumbeswarar Temple

The temple is dedicated to the Lord Shiva. Lord Shiva is worshiped as Adi Kumbeswarar and is represented by the lingam. His consort Parvati is depicted as Mangalambigai Amman. A glorious architecture structure spread out on 8 acres land. The tallest is the eastern tower, with 11 stories and a height of 128 feet. The present masonry structure was built during the Cholas dynasty in the 9th century, while later expansions are attributed to Vijayanagar rulers of the Thanjavur Nayaks of the 16th century which reminds us of our glorious past. It is located at a distance of 40 kilometres from Thanjavur. It is 1.9 kilometers from kumbakonam.

Visiting Timings:-6am to 12:30pm and 4pm to 9:30 pm

 

Sarangapani Temple

It is one of 108 Divya Desams.This is a Vishnu Temple where Vishnu is found reclined on the adiseshan. This is one of the 108 Divya Desams of Lord Vishnu.One of the oldest Vaishnavite Temples in South India. The Gopuram of the temple is an excellent example for architectural marvels of our country.The whole temple looks to be drawn by chariot with beautifully carved huge elephants on either side.This exquisitely carved huge temple houses amazing statue of lord Vishnu in a reclining pose. Outside it, His consort Komalavalli (goddess Lakshmi) temple exists. Unlike normal temples there is no direct entry but from sides either south or north depending on the solar movement-Dakshinayana or Uttarayana.One should not miss this ancient temple in the itinerary. It is 1.6 kilometers from kumbakonam.

Visiting Timings:-7am to 12pm and 5pm to 9pm

 

Ramaswamy Temple

Although temple from outside looks small but after entering it is very huge and has plenty of pillars carved intricately, it has a huge corridor and has carving on the outer walls of corridor also.This temple is famous for Ramayana inscriptions on the walls, all the four outer walls are covered with various scenes of Ramayana. The sculptures in the inner temple are also very nice, it is situated near Adikkum Eswara swamy temple. It is 1.9 kilometers from kumbakonam.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM to 9PM

 

Kasi Viswanathar Temple

The temple is unlike other temples and has a great history behind it. One who visits this temple must visit the nearby pond and take a bath in the pond. It is believed that if you get a bath in the pond you will be washed of sins and get sacred. The pond is magnificent and the city is very beautiful and you can find a lot of temples nearby worth visiting.Situated right beside the Mahamaham tank, it is a visual treat for the eyes and mind. Especially the various rivers in the form deities is a specialty. It is always the first temple we visit thanks to its proximity to the hotel. Sadly the temple does not attract much crowd except during Mahamaham festival. It is 1.2 kilometers from kumbakonam.

Visiting Timings:-6.00am to 12pm and 4pm to 9pm

 

Thiruvidaimarudur

It is significant to the Hindu sect of Shaivism as one of the seven major Shiva temples. Shiva is worshiped as Mahalingeswaraswamy, and is represented by the lingam, with his idol referred to as Jothiramalingam. His consort Parvati is depicted as Pirgu Chuntaragujambigai. Nice temple with availability of pooja materials in the front.Vastly constructed with large gopurams, going around the temple will be a huge challenge as there are no guides.People who are suffering from incurable mental diseases get cured by just visiting and praying in the temple. It is 8.5 kilometers from kumbakonam.

Visiting Timings:-9am to 5pm

 

Patteeswaram

Patteeswaram is located around 8 km from Kumbakonam and is famous for the Sri Durgai Shrine and a Shiva Temple called the Sri Dhenupureeswarar Temple. It is believed that Sri Durga, also known as Vishnu Durga, was placed in the Durgai Temple by the Cholas. The Cholas brought the idol to this place and built a separate shrine for the goddess. The idol of Sri Durga has three eyes and eight hands. The idol stands over the body of Mahishasura, the famous demon king. It is 7.7 kilometers from kumbakonam.

Visiting Timings:-6am to 1pm and 4pm to 9pm and Friday:6am to 1pm and 4pm to 10pm

 

Brahma Temple

Normally we do not find much temples for Lord Brahma. Here we have an unique temple which is clean and less crowded.Here lord Brahma idol has four faces and the face which is facing back is a female face which has to be seen in the mirror. One has to request the temple priest to show the fourth face of Brahma in the mirror.This temple is behind Ramaswamy temple. It is 1.9 kilometers from kumbakonam.

Visiting Timings:-8am to 11:30am and 5:30PM to 9:00PM
