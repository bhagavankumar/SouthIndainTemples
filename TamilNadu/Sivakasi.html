Historical Sites Around Sivakasi

 

Thiruvilliputhur Andal Kovil

Thiruvilliputhur Andal Kovil or Srivilliputtur Vatapatrasayi Temple or Thiruvilliputhur Divya Desam is one of the 108 Divya Deshams or the holy abodes of Lord Vishnu. This temple is located at Srivilliputtur town, which is about 20 km from Sivakashi en route Virudhunagar. It is 21.1 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-7:00 AM - 1:00 AM and 4:00 PM - 9 PM

 

Sri Badrakaliamman Temple

Sri Badrakaliamman Temple is the most famous temple in Sivakasi. This temple is dedicated to Goddess Badrakaliamman, who is also the patron goddess of the town. The main feature of this temple is its ‘rajagopuram’, which is considered to be the highest amongst all the Kali temple gopuras of Tamilnadu. It is 1.3 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-9:00 AM - 6:00 PM

 

Swamy Kasi Viswanatha Thiru Koil

Swamy Kasi Viswanatha Thiru Koil located in Sivakasi is dedicated to Kasi Vishwanatha (Lord Shiva of Kasi) and Visalatchi Ambal.Being one of the most famous and ancient temples of Tamil Nadu, it is believed that King Harikesari Parakkirama Pandian, who was the ruler of Southern Madurai, bought a Shiva lingam from Kasi and worshipped it in his kingdom. The temple was expanded by the later rulers, who began calling it Kasi Viswanatha Swamy Temple. The shrine has seen numerous reconstruction and development ever since.Ayyanar Falls, Pilavakkal Dam, Kullur Sandhai Reservoir, Sri Badrakaliamman Temple, Parasakthi Mari Amman Temple, Sri Venkatachalapathy Temple, Kachakara Amman Koil, Perumal Koil and the Pathira Kali Amman Thirukkoil are some of the most famous tourist attractions of Sivakasi that tourist must visit while visiting the Swamy Kasi Vishwanatha Thiru Koil. It is 55 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-6AM–12PM and 4PM–9PM

 

Parasakthi Mari Amman Temple

Parasakthi Mari Amman Temple is one of the main attractions of Sivakashi town. Built in 1923, the temple is a popular pilgrim spot in Shivakashi. Panguni Uttiram festival is the main celebration in this temple which is celebrated for 21 days. It is 26.6 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-9am to 7pm

 

Tiruthangal

Tiruthangal is one of the 108 sacred abodes of Lord Vishnu, located at Shivakashi en route Virudhunagar. Ninranarayanaperumal Kovil, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, was constructed during the rule of Early Pandyas. Later the temple was renovated in the 10th century and was re-constructed during the period of the Nayaks.It is also believed that the three Sangam age poets - Mudakkorranar, Porkollan Vennahanar and Athireyan Sengannanar also lived at Tiruthangal. It is 4.5 kilometers from kanchipuram.

Visiting Timings:-9:00 AM to 6 PM
