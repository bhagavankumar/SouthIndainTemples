Historical Sites Around Sriperumbudur

 

Vallakottai Murugan Temple

Vallakottai Murugan temple is around 1200 years old and is situated in the Vallakottai Village. The prime attraction of the temple is the 7 ft long statue of Lord Murugan, which is the tallest idol of the deity in Tamil Nadu. The statues of consorts of Lord Murugan, namely Teyvayanai and Valli, are placed on either side of the statue of Lord Murugan.

According to the mythology, the temple was constructed by King Bhagareethan. Advised by Sage Dhurvasur, the king worshipped Lord Murugan to get back his kingdom. Another legend states that Vallan, a demon, used to torture the Devas. On request of Devas, Lord Murugan, defeated the demon and brought peace to the region.

Beautiful carvings of Thapas Kamakshi and Lord Rama embracing Sri Anjaneyar are carved on the pillars of Ardha Mandapam. The Thai Poosam, Skanda Sashti, Tamil New Year’s Day and Aadi Krithikai are some of the festivals celebrated at the Vallakottai Murugan Temple. It is 10.2 kilometers from sriperumbudur.
