Historical Sites Around Thiruvannamalai

 

Arunachaleswara Temple

This temple is one of the most important temples located in the Arunachala Hills of Thiruvannamalai. The temple dedicated to one of the several forms of Lord Shiva that is Arunachaleswarar, is considered one of the holy elements or Panchabhoota. The other four temples located here are Kalahasti (wind), Tiruvanaikka (water), Kanchipuram (Earth) and Chidambaram (Space).The temple consists of pillars decorated with ancient scriptures and images that of the ancient era. The inscriptions on the walls are mostly of the ancient rules and history of the place. Major festivals celebrated here include the Krithika Deepam, which is a light festival celebrated in the month of Krithika and Thiruvoodal festival. It is 1.1 kilometers from thiruvannamalai.

Visiting Timings:-5:30 AM -9:30 PM

 

Ramana Ashram

This ashram located in Thiruvannamalai dedicated to Saint Ramana who is said to have attained Samadhi at this place. This ashram displays all the important information, philosophies, teachings and facts related to the life of Maharshi Ramana. As per the legends, Saint Ramana attained vision related to supreme truth of life here. This ashram also has an ancient temple dedicated to lord Shiva that was carved out of caves by Pallavas. The ashram also carries out many social services. It is 2.4 kilometers from thiruvannamalai.

Visiting Timings:-6:30 AM -9:00 PM

 

Gingee Fort

Gingee Fort, which is also known by the name of Chenji Fort, is the only ancient fort that still stands in the region of Tamil Nadu. Located in the district of Villupuram, the fort was also referred to as the ‘Troy of the East’ by the British. Reference of the fort is also mentioned in the history as the fort praised by Chatrapati Shivaji himself. He ranked this fort as ‘one of the most impregnable fortress of India’.The real Gingee fort that was built by the Chola Dynasty in the 9th century was relatively smaller than the current fort, which was rebuilt later on in the 13th century. The present form of the fort was attributed to the effort of Shivaji himself. However, the fort is not in proper conditions now but the architectural beauty is still worth seeing. It is 39.4 kilometers from thiruvannamalai.

Visiting Timings:-9:30am to 4:30 pm

 

Seshadri Swamigal Ashram

Close to the Ramana Ashram is the sacred spot of Seshadri Swamigal Ashram, which is dedicated to the popular 20th century Saint Seshadri Swamigal who used to display his mystical powers during that time in order to bless the devotees who attended his Ashram. Most of religious rituals of this ashram are conducted in the garden located nearby which is also a favourable spot for meditation. Accommodation options for a comfortable stay are also available for the travellers. It is 2.2 kilometers from thiruvannamalai.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM -9:00 PM

 

Skandashramam

Located on the northern side of the Arunachala hill is the Skandashramam. This ashram is in the town of Thiruvannamalai near to the temple of Arunchaleswara and Ramana Ashram. Skandashramam is believed to be one of the ancient caves where Saint Ramana stayed during his visit to Thiruvannamalai. Travellers have to undertake a long trek in order to reach the cave of Skandashramam where disciples of Shri Ramana Maharshi recite prayers and meditate. Large number of devotees and travellers all round the year visits this ashram. It is 2.2 kilometers from thiruvannamalai.

Visiting Timings:-9:00 AM to 5:15 PM
