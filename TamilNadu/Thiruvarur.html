Historical Sites Around Thiruvarur

 

Sri Thyagarajaswamy Temple

Sri Thyagarajaswamy Temple is dedicated to Somaskandar, a form of Lord Shiva. The temple complex has shrines of other gods, such as the Kamalaamba, Vanmikanathar and Thyagarajar. It also has a Kamalalayam temple tank which is considered to be the largest in India.The Thyagaraja car festival is celebrated at this temple once in 12 years, along with the Kumbabishekam. This festival is known as Azhazhi Ther and is celebrated in the month of April. It is 1.3 kilometers from Thruvarur.

Visiting Timings:- 5am to 12pm and 4pm to 9pm.

 

Pathanjali Manoharar Temple

It is located at a place called Vilamal, about 4 km from Thiruvarur. It is believed that Saint Patanjali happened to have come there. It is a Shiva temple and his concert here is called Madhura Bhashini. It also has shrines for Ganesha, Subramanya and Bhairava. Swamy Chandrashekhar Shivacharya is at present residing there who is a saint with immense knowledge on Philosophy. It is 3.8 kilometers from Thruvarur.

Visiting Timings:-9am to 6pm.

 

Saraswati Temple

The Saraswati Temple, situated in Kuthanur village on the banks of the Arasilar River, is devoted to Goddess Saraswati, the consort of Lord Brahma. Local people believe that during the 12th century, a Tamil poet, Ottakuttar, meditated to Goddess Saraswati for creating this temple at Kuthanur. At the entrance of the temple, there is an image of Ottakuttar welcoming the devotees. It is 20 kilometers from Thruvarur.

Visiting Timings:-6am to 1pm and 4pm to 9pm.

 

Sri Rajagopalaswamy Temple

Sri Rajagopalaswamy Temple, situated in Mannargudi, is dedicated to Rajagopalaswamy, an incarnation of Lord Krishna. It is a Vaishnavite shrine and covers an area of around 23 acres.The temple has a 12 ft tall idol of the main deity. It also has 24 shrines, 7 mandapams, 9 theerthams, 16 gopurams and 7 prakarams that were constructed by Kulothunga Chola I.These structures were renovated by the subsequent Chola rulers and the Thanjavur Nayaks. In the month of March, Par Pughalum Panguni Tiruvizha is celebrated at the temple. It is 29.5 kilometers from Thruvarur.

Visiting Timings:-5am to 1pm and 4pm to 9:30pm.
