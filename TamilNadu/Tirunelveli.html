Historical Sites Around Tirunelveli

 

Swamy Nellaiappar Temple And Kanthimathi Ambal Temple

Swamy Nellaiappar Temple and Kanthimathi Ambal Temple is located at a distance of around 2 km from the railway station. The temple has been constructed as per the rules written in agama shastras by Rama Pandian. Various important parts of the temple are believed to have been built by the famous ruler Nindrasir Nedumaran of the 17th century.

A chain mandapam is located within the premises of this structure along with a flower garden, which is assumed to have been built in 1756. The major attraction of this garden is the square vasantha mandapam that has around 100 pillars. It is 5 kilometers from tirunelveli.

Visiting Timings:-5.30 AM to 12.30 PM and 04.00 PM to 08.30 PM.

 

Papanasam

Papanasam is a village located in the Tirunelveli District and has a famous temple called the Papanasam Temple. Papavinaseswarar, an incarnation of Lord Shiva, is the presiding deity of this temple.

Another attraction of this village is the Papanasam Dam, also known as the Thamirabarani River Dam. It is believed to have been built during the British era and is surrounded by high mountains and huge trees. The dam is about 240 m high, 5.4 m wide and 265 m long and has a total catchment area of around 147 sq km.

Monkeys of different breeds can be spotted around this dam. Boating can be done in the nearby lake. It is 60 kilometers from tirunelveli.

Visiting Timings:-6:30 am to 1 pm and 6:30 pm to 8:00 pm.

 

Tenkasi

Tenkasi is known as the Varanasi of the southern region of India. This place is famous for a temple known as the Vishwanatha Temple, devoted to Lord Shiva. The temple is around 500 years old and belongs to the era of the Pandya Dynasty. The major attractions of this temple are its various images, inscriptions and pillars. It is 60.9 kilometers from tirunelveli.

Visiting Timings:-6am to 12 pm and 4pm to 8:30 pm.

 

Srivaikuntam

Srivaikuntam is a temple dedicated to one of the forms of Lord Vishnu, known as Srivaikuntapathi Swami. The temple has a gopuram and mandapam that are decorated with numerous sculptures of elephants, yallis and warriors. Another mandapam called the Ekadasi Mandapam is also adorned with various beautiful sculptures.

This temple is believed to have served as a fort during the days of conflict between the East India Company and Kattabomman, a chieftain from Panchalankurichi. The Kailasanathaswamy Temple is famous for the six inscriptions that belong to the period of Veera Pandiya Kattabomman. It is 2.4 kilometers from tirunelveli.

Visiting Timings:-7:30am to 12pm and 5pm to 8pm.

 

Nanguneri

Nanguneri is a taluk headquarters and is popular for the Totadrinathar Temple, also known as the Totadri Mutt. It is managed by the Vanamamalai Mutt and Thothathri Nathan is the presiding deity of this temple. An oil well, called the Ennai Kinaru, is located within the premises of this temple.

This place is enclosed by dense forests, hamlets and the Western Ghats. This region is counted among the divya sheathram. The major attractions are the festivals of Panguni and Chaitra, celebrated from March through April and April through May, respectively. It is 33 kilometers from tirunelveli.

Visiting Timings:-8 am to 12 pm and 5 pm to 9 pm.

 

Ambasamudram

Ambasamudram is located on the foothills of the Western Ghats in Tirunelveli. It is situated on the shores of River Thamirabarani and is a fertile agricultural area. This town is popular for wooden handicrafts and the Sri Sorimuthu Iyyanar Temple, dedicated to Sastha. On the full moon day of the month of Aadi, a festival of traditional durbar is celebrated for two days. It is 6 kilometers from tirunelveli Junction.

Visiting Timings:-Visiting Timings:-6am to 11am and 5pm to 7:30pm.

 

Pattamadai

Pattamadai is situated on the banks of River Thamirabarani and is known for the Pattamadai Kora Mat. The mat has been developed using Kora grass and is believed to be the birthplace of Swami Sivananda Saraswati, a philosopher and saint. It is 13 kilometers from tirunelveli.

Visiting Timings:-7 am to 11:30 am and 5:30 pm - 8 pm.

 

Palaivananathar Temple

The Palaivananathar Temple is devoted to Lord Palaivananathar and Goddess Thavalavennagaiammai. It is situated in Thirupparaithurai and has a theertham called the Kaveri. It is 6 kilometers from tirunelveli railway station.

Visiting Timings:-6am to 11am and 4:30pm to 8:30 pm

 

Kailasanathar Temple

The Kailasanathar Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva in the form of Sri Kailasanathar Swamy. The temple also has an idol of Goddess Brahannayaki. It is believed to have been constructed during the reign of Raja Raja Chola. The Hoysala and Pandya kings are associated with the reconstruction of the temple.

The temple has a seven storeyed rajagopuram and a five storeyed back gopuram. The separate sanctum of this temple has various Shivalingams, namely Sri Badari Vaneswarar, Sri Arunachaleswarar, Sri Sundareswarar and Sri Kailasanathar.

Another attraction of this temple is the Pradosha Sabhai that has idols of Pradosha and Ashta Dikpalakas, along with their apsaras, vahanas and Shiva Boothaganas. It also has shrines of Goddess Saraswati, Lord Chandra, Lord Surya, Lord Murugan, Goddess Brahannayaki and Lord Ganesha. A huge Nandi bull, somawara mandapam and arudra mandapam reflect the beautiful architectural works. It is 8 kilometers from tirunelveli.

Visiting Timings:-5:30AM–12PM and 4PM–9PM.
