Historical Sites Around Tranquebar

 

Town Gate

Town Gate on the King's Street is also known as the Landporten in Danish and is a part of the fortification done during the 1660s. The original gate was destroyed in 1791 and a new gate was constructed in its place.

The town gate is the entrance for the Tranquebar (Tarangambadi). The leading street through this gate is the King's street which takes us to the beach area. King's street is the main street of Tranquebar. On the way, are the churches(Zion church, New Jerusalem Church) and other buildings. The gate itself is treated as a monument. The gate house, a property run by Neemrana, is an option to stay and is situated right opposite to the Town gate on the King's street. It is 0.9 kilometer from traquebar.

Visiting Timings:-10AM–5:45PM

 

Fort Dansborg

Fort Dansborg, the 17th century Danish Fort, was built immediately after the arrival of the Danish trading expedition in India. It was the result of a treaty signed between the Danish East Indian Company and Nayak Raghunatha in Thanjavur in 1620. Till the end of the 17th century, the fort was used for storage and residential purposes.

Since its establishment, the fort has been renovated several times by both Danish non-profit organisations as well as Indian volunteers. There is a museum inside the fort that displays various artefacts belonging to the Danish settlement in India. It is 1 kilometer from traquebar.

Visiting Timings:-10AM–5:45PM

 

The Zion Church

The Zion Church on the King's Street, sanctified in 1701, is considered to be the oldest protestant church in India. It is 1 kilometer from traquebar.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM-9:30 AM and 11:00 AM-6:15 PM

 

The New Jerusalem Church

The New Jerusalem Church, situated on the King's Street was constructed in 1718 by Rev. Bartholomaeus Ziegenbalg. This church was established after the arrival of German missionaries in India, as the existing church proved to be small for the rising Christian population. It was established mainly for the converted Indians. The church has a small cemetery that has tombstones belonging to the 18th century. It is 1.5 kilometer from traquebar.

Visiting Timings:-7:00 AM -7:00 PM

 

Masilamani Nathar Koil

Masilamani Nathar Koil, near the Bungalow on the Beach, was constructed during the Pandian era in 1306. An inscription regarding the construction of the temple is on display in the Dansborg Museum. It is 1 kilometer from traquebar.

Visiting Timings:-8:30 AM to 11 AM and 5:30PM to 8 PM

 

Ziegenbalg Museum Complex

Ziegenbalg Museum Complex on the Admiral Street has the first printing press of India. The press was originally started in the Mission House on King's Street. The first book published in India, a Bible in Tamil, was printed here. It is 0.8 kilometer from traquebar.

Visiting Timings:-10AM–1PM and 2PM–5:45PM

 

Danish Museum

Danish Museum, situated inside the Fort Dansborg, has various artefacts related to the colonial period and Danish settlement in India. The museum is managed by Dr. T. S. Sridhar, IAS Principal Secretary and Commissioner of Archaeology. Glass objects, porcelain ware, steatite lamps, decorated terracotta objects, Danish manuscripts, Chinese tea jars and lamps are displayed in the museum. It is 0.9 kilometer from traquebar.

Visiting Timings:-10AM–5:45PM

 

Thirukkadaiyur

Thirukkadaiyur, a small temple town situated close to Tranquebar. This town is known for the ancient Amritaghateswarar-Abirami temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. This 11th century temple was constructed by Raja Chola of the Chola Dynasty.

The temple complex has three holy water tanks, namely the Kaala Theertham, Markandeya Theertham and Amrita Pushkarini. Pilgrims bath here before entering the temple. Thirkkadaiyur is also known for important tourist attractions, like the Mohamed Bunder, Ammanpettai, Thiruppoonthuruth, Thuillaisthanam and Kaduveli. It is 1 kilometer from traquebar.

Visiting Timings:-6am-1pm and 4pm-9pm
