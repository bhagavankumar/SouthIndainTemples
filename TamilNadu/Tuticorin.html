Historical Sites Around Tuticorin

 

Manapad

Manapad is one of the religious attractions at Tuticorin, which is situated at about 60 km away. Manapad is a coastal village famous for its Holy Cross Church. This is a famous church in Tamil Nadu, which has Saint Francis Xavier as the patron Saint. It is 51.2 kilometers from tuticorin.

Visiting Timings:-8am to 12pm and 6:30pm to 10pm

 

Our Lady Of Snows Basilica

This church is very famous and located right in front of the port of Tuticorin and data goes back saying Our lady Of Snow statue is brought from Italy and is very special. The sand beach surrounding the church is feel good factor and also has great connection to sailors. The golden chariot in this church is taken out as procession once in every five years, which is grand event in the town and it gathers crowd from various parts of the globe. It is 1.2 kilometers from tuticorin.

Visiting Timings:-5:30am to 6pm

 

Tiruchendur Temple

Tiruchendur temple, known popularly as Thiruchendur Murugan Kovil, is one of the main destinations at Tuticorin. Thiruchendur Murugan Kovil, situated in Thiruchendur, is one of the Aaru Padai Veedu of Lord Muruga. This ancient temple, which dates back to the Vedic Period, is also noted for its architectural marvel.

Tiruchendur temple is famous for the auspicious occasions like Brahmothsavam, Vasanthothsavam, Visaka Visakam, Skandha Sashti, Aavanipperunthirunaal and Maasipperunthirunaal and Oonjal Sevai. It is 2 kilometers from tuticorin.

Visiting Timings:-5AM–9PM

 

Panchalankurichi

Panchalankurichi is another historic attraction offered by Tuticorin. Panchalankurichi, situated about 18 km away from Tuticorin, is the birthplace of Veerapandiya Kattabomman, a 17th century Palayakarrar, who fought against the British.

Panchalankurichi is famous for the Kattabomman Memorial Fort, built in 1947 by the Government of Tamil Nadu, which is maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India. It is 33.6 kilometers from tuticorin.

Visiting Timings:-8AM–1PM and 2PM–6PM

 

Ottapidaram

Ottapidaram is a famous historic place in Tamil Nadu, at Tuticorin. Ottapidaram was the birth place of the famous freedom fighter, V.O, Chidambaram. The house in which he lived has been converted in to a memorial. It is 28.3 kilometers from tuticorin.

Visiting Timings:-8AM–1PM and 2PM–6PM

 

Kalugumalai

Kalugumalai is one of the popular destinations. Kalugumalai is famous for its ancient rock cut Jain cave temple. Several rock-cut images of Adinatha, Neminatha, Mahaveera, Parvanatha and Bahubali can also be found at Kalugumalai.

Kalugumalai also has Vattuvan Koil, Murugan temple and an unfinished 8th century Pandyan monolith cave temple. Vettuvan Kovil is the only monolithic Pandyan Kovil which has survived the rampage of time. It is 22 kilometers from tuticorin.

Visiting Timings:-5.30 AM - 12.30 PM and 6.00 PM - 9.00 PM

 

Sri Vaikuntam Temple

Sri Vaikuntam (Vaikuntanathan) temple is one of the famous pilgrimage destinations atTuticorin. Sri Vaikuntam temple is considered among 108 Divyadesams, which were patronized by the Azhvaars as well as among the Navatirupathi, the temples situated on both the banks of River Tamiraibarani.

Sri Vaikuntam or Vaikuntanathan temple, dedicated to Lord Kallarpiran or Vaikuntanathan (Vishnu), is situated about 30 km from Tirunelveli on Tiruchendur road. The sanctum sanctorum of the temple features serpent Lord Adisesha unfurling his hood over the standing idol of Lord Vishnu, which is considered one of its kind among all the 108 Divyadeshams.

The temple, built by the Pandya King Sankara Pandyan, is noted for its traditional South Indian architecture. The temple is also known for its unique feature, wherein the Sun's rays falls directly on the idol of Lord Vaikuntanathan on two days every year. It is 39.1 kilometers from tuticorin.

Visiting Timings:-6.00 AM to 12.30 PM and 5.00 PM to 8.30 PM
