Historical Sites Around Vellore

 

Sripuram Golden Temple

Sripuram Golden Temple is a Mahalakshmi temple, which was inaugurated on 24th August, 2007. The temple covering and the sculptures are made of more than 1500 kg of pure gold.Spread over an area of 4,04,685.642 sq m, this temple is one of the largest golden temples in the world. It was constructed by the Vellore-based Sri Narayani Peedam.

The spiritual leader, Sri Sakthi Amma, who is also known as Narayani Amma, heads this temple. Located eight kilometres from the Vellore Bus Terminus, the temple is well connected. It is 12.3 kilometers from vellore.

Visiting Timings:-4am to 7pm

 

Vellore Fort

The Vellore Fort, formerly the headquarters of the Vijayanagara Empire, is a large 16th-century fort in Vellore. The administration of this fort passed from the Vijayanagara kings to the Bijapur Sultans. From them, this fort went into the hands of the Marathas and then to the Carnatic Nawabs. Later the fort went under the administration of the British.

Sprawling over an area of 5,38,231.90 sq m, the fort houses a temple, mosque and church, along with several other buildings. This fort has one of the best military architectures in Southern India.The fort is also known for its ramparts, moat and masonry. Sri Jalakandeswarar Temple which lies within the fort, is known for its sculptures. Dedicated to Jalakandeswarar, this temple has a double gopuram and mandapam.

This fort also includes Muthu Mandapam, which is a memorial, built around the tombstone of Sri Vikrama Rajasinha. The fort also houses a church and a mosque that were constructed during the early British period and the last Arcod Nawab's period, respectively.The Government Museum inside this fort displays objects from anthropology, botany, geology, numismatics, pre-history, and zoology. It is 1.4 kilometers from vellore.

Virinjipuram Temple

This is both historically and from a religious point of view a very good place to visit. The architecture is simply amazing. I suggest to read up about the temple before the visit to truly appreciate it. There is a tunnel from a nearby fort connecting here thats almost 5 km long. The gopurams here really depict our history. The aarti is really a sight to behold. A must for a person visiting the city. It is 16 kilometers from vellore railway station.

Visiting Timings:-6am to 11am and 4pm to 8pm

 

Jalakandeswarar Temple

The Jalakandeswarar Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, who is worshipped here in the form of Jalakandeswarar. The temple is named Jalakandeswarar, as it is located at a lower height than the temple moat.This temple was once closed for a very long time, when its main deity was taken away to a distant location. According to the locals, the deity was brought back in 1980 when the region suffered from a severe water scarcity. It is 1.6 kilometers from vellore.

Visiting Timings:-6:30am to 1pm and 6 pm to 8:30pm

 

Chelliamman Kovil

This is a very popular temple in the city that is visited everyday by thousands. The temple is well maintained and the lines for darshan are also well organized. It is near the bus stand and on the banks of the river Palar. A nice place to go with family. It is 4.5 kilometers from vellore.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM -8:00 PM

 

Sri Murugan Temple

Visited this holy temple namely Arulmigu Balamurugan Tirukkovil at Village Rathinagiri. The temple is on Kanchi-Vellore Highway just 10-12 km before Vellore.Ratnagiri Balamurugan Temple is an ancient Murugan Temple. It was built around the 14th century. It is situated on top of a hill. Ancient Hindu scriptures say where there is a hill, there is Lord Murugan. They take nominal entry fees of Rs. 5 from each pilgrims. Must visit place when you are at Vellore. It is 4.6 kilometers from vellore.

Visiting Timings:-6:00 AM -8:00 PM

 

State Government Museum

The State Government Museum is a prominent tourist attraction within the Vellore Fort. This museum was opened in 1985. The museum holds a display of numerous objects of art, archaeology, pre-history, weapons and sculptures. There are also exhibits of wood carvings, handicrafts, numismatics, philately, botany, geology and zoology.

One of the galleries in the museum holds a display of the historical monuments of the composite North Arcot District. The museum also has an exhibit of a bronze double antenna sword, which belongs to the Vellore Taluk and dates back to around 400 BC.

Among other exhibits of the museum are stone sculptures from the late Pallava to the Vijayanagar periods and ivory chess boards and coins that were used by Vikrama Raja Singha. It is 1.7 kilometers from vellore.

Visiting Timings:-9:00 AM -6:00 PM
